# Narration ↔ Visual Alignment

## Core rule

When narration is speaking about a concrete thing, the viewer should usually be seeing that thing, its result, or a clearly supportive visual at the same moment.

Do not let the voiceover describe one idea while the screen is showing an unrelated idea unless the mismatch is intentional and clearly effective.

## What counts as aligned

A narration line is aligned if the concurrent visual does at least one of these:
- literally shows the noun or event being mentioned
- shows a close causal consequence of the line
- shows a symbolic but obvious equivalent
- sets up the line immediately before it lands, then pays it off on the key word

## What counts as misaligned

Misalignment examples:
- voice says rain / storm / wet track while the shot is dry and sunny
- voice says close-up product detail while the shot is a far wide with no readable product
- voice says crowd roar / launch / impact while the shot is visually static
- voice says speed / acceleration while the shot feels slow or disconnected

## Shot selection rule

For each narration line, decide its strongest visual partner before locking the timeline.

Write a simple mapping in `edit-plan.md`:
- line summary
- target shot or subclip
- why it matches

If a line and shot do not match well, fix the narrowest layer first:
1. move the line to a better shot
2. retime the cut so the keyword lands on the right image
3. swap the shot
4. rewrite the line
5. regenerate only if needed

## Timing rule

The most important word in the line should land on the clearest matching visual if possible.

Examples:
- "rain hits the visor" -> show rain / visor / droplets right on "rain" or "visor"
- "the lights go out" -> cut to launch or start-light payoff on "out"
- "the can snaps open" -> show can close-up and opening action on "snaps open"

## Preflight rule

Before final render, inspect every narration segment and ask:
- what is the line actually saying?
- what is the viewer seeing during that line?
- would a human say "yes, that matches"?

If not, block render until fixed.

## Delivery truthfulness

Do not describe the edit as polished if narration/visual sync is still obviously mismatched.
